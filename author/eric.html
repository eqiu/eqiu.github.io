Finals Betting Arbitrage

Published: Sat 27 May 2017
By Eric

In Thoughts.

tags: betting arbitrage

First off, a brief summary of how betting lines work. Below are the betting prices for the NBA finals series. These prices are from 888east.com taken on 5-27-2017 at around 2 pm CST. Note that betting lines will vary from site to site and do not remain constant throughout time.
There are two outcomes "Cavs Win" (+215) and "Warriors Win" (-270) with attached prices respectively. These prices mean that a 100-dollar bet on the Cavs would result in a 215-dollar return if the Cavs won. On the flip side, it would take a 270-dollar bet on the Warriors for a 100-dollar return if the Warriors won. What is really interesting is to compare these percentages to the Finals MVP odds. Below is of all of the Finals MVP odds (same timestamp) and the percentages of each can also be calculated using the same metric as above.
For all intensive purposes we will focus on Lebron James and his odds, for which I have recreated a smaller table below and added in the relevant percentages:

Player Odds Winning Percent
Lebron James +125 44.4%

Notice something weird? Lebron has higher odds of winning finals MVP than the Cavs do of winning the championship. It is basically impossible for a finals MVP winner to come from a losing team. Since the NBA awarded the award in 1969, only Jerry West (the inaugural recipient) has won the finals MVP when his team lost in the finals. That's 47 finals in a row with the MVP coming from the winning side and 11 game 7's in a row. Looking specifically at the odds Lebron (44.4% to win finals mvp) has more than a 12% edge on the Cavs (31.7% of winning finals). In an ideal setting, one could bet on the Cavs winning the finals and sell an equal amount on Lebron winning MVP. The only risk in this situation would be a recreation of 1969. Here is a closer examination of the potential returns if a bettor put 100-dollars short on Lebron Finals MVP and 100-dollars long on Cavs winning the finals:

Outcome Long Return Short Return Total Return
Warriors win, Lebron not MVP -100 +100 0
Warriors win, Lebron MVP -100 -125 -225
Cavs win, Lebron MVP +215 -125 +90
Cavs win, Lebron not MVP +215 +100 +315

I personally believe that of the four scenarios a Warriors win, Lebron MVP is the least likely. There however is one glaring issue. Previously, I specified the word ideal, because it is not possible to make this bet. Unlike the financial markets, sports bettors cannot sell a line short, only book keepers can do that, thus rendering it impossible to take advantage of this arbitrage. If it were possible for individuals to sell lines, I believe this arbitrage would not exist and either Lebron MVP odds would drop or Cavs series odds would rise or both might happen and meet at an equilibrium.

TLDR: There is a discrepancy between the actual NBA finals mvp odds and the series winner odds. I grabbed screenshots with the same timestamps of these two lines to create a hypothetical bet that is almost risk-free, but unable to be carried out in todays gambling market.
